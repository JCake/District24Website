Beyond Your Club: Become a Sponsor, Mentor, or Coach!

Step outside your club to help grow other clubs and enhance your leadership skills.  Consider taking on one of these exciting roles.

A club sponsor helps form a new club by generating interest and educating about the benefits of Toastmasters.  See the Toastmasters presentation on sponsors.

A club mentor helps a new club succeed by teaching best practices, answering questions, and guiding the new club to make the most of the Toastmasters experience.  See the Toastmasters presentation on mentoring.

A club coach helps an existing club with 12 or fewer members succeed by helping them discover new ways they can increase membership and improve meeting quality.  More info on coaching at https://www.toastmasters.org/ClubCoachProgram and in our training video: 

Email cgd@d24tm.org for more information or to volunteer.

Club Growth Tactics 

(Snippet)

Guest connection and follow-up

  • Use the new “Lead Management” system

    • Respond promptly to requests from “Find a Club”

  • Reach out to guests via an email or phone call (call preferred so they can ask questions)

  • Give guests a choice of “two yeses”: "I invite you to join today. Did you want to go ahead and do that now? Or do you want to connect with Jane now and go over your questions about joining?" (From Founders District CGD April Braswell)

Marketing and promotion

  • Create a Google Business Profile

  • Use Search Engine Optimization to increase the number of visitors to your club’s website

  • Create a YouTube channel for posting promos and/or speeches

  • Create a LinkedIn page

  • Email prm@d24tm.org about listing your club’s meetings on District 24’s Meetup page or create your club’s own Meetup page

  • Explore the “Club Membership Approved Promos” folder at https://d24tm.org/downloads.html
