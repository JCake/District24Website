Speechcraft Program

Have you heard about Speechcraft?  The Speechcraft program empowers your club to build membership by presenting an engaging course for non-Toastmasters who want to grow their communication skills.  Participants completing the course may find they have more to learn and choose to join your club as members.  Coordinating a Speechcraft also earns you credit towards your Distinguished Toastmaster Award.

Questions and Answers

Several questions are answered at https://www.toastmasters.org/education/speechcraft/speechcraft-faq, and our District has some additional questions and answers for you:

What is Speechcraft?

Speechcraft is a short-term educational program offered by Toastmasters International. It provides participants with an introduction to Toastmasters by focusing on essential communication and leadership skills. Speechcraft allows participants to experience the Toastmasters environment before committing to full membership.

Who teaches the Speechcraft course?

  • Speechcraft must be presented by a Toastmasters club.  One facilitator manages the program.  (Or two co-faciliators if you combine two bundles into one class.)

  • Club officers and experienced members assist in various roles, including evaluating speeches, mentoring participants, and helping coordinate sessions.

Does a club member have to have reached a specific Pathways Level in order to be the Speechcraft Coordinator?

While there is no specific Pathways Level required, because the Coordinator will be delivering directed learning to adults, it is recommended that the Coordinator be an experienced public speaker. 

How many participants are in a Speechcraft course?

  • Each Speechcraft Digital Bundle ($50) includes licenses for five participants and one facilitator.

  • Additional participants require additional bundles, but you don't need to use all the participant slots.  For example, you could host an 8-person class with two bundles, two co-faciliators, and four students assigned per bundle.

When should Speechcraft classes be scheduled?

For easy scheduling, you may wish to conduct Speechcraft during the club’s regularly scheduled meeting times.  This helps participants acclimate to the club’s environment and schedule, making the transition to full membership smoother once the program concludes.

You may also offer Speechcraft as a standalone experience seperate from your club's established meeting time.

How many sessions/classes should there be in a single Speechcraft course? 

4-8 sessions/classes is typical, but not required.  Some have even found a way to do Speechcraft in a Day.  Create the schedule that will work best for your targeted participants.  

Can remote clubs offer Speechcraft? 

While the Speechcraft Overview (Form 493) references in-person sessions along with on-line materials, the club has flexibility with the delivery method.

Does a club charge Speechcraft participants? 

The club has complete flexibility about whether or not to charge participants.  Some clubs offer the Speechcraft series for free, some pass on the $10 fee directly, some add additional fees for other expenses.  For clubs that charge a fee, some clubs offer to apply those fees to the initial Toastmasters dues if the participant joins the club.

What recognition does a Speechcraft coordinator receive?

Conducting Speechcraft fulfills the “supporting Toastmasters growth” requirement for the DTM award.

What benefits does Speechcraft provide for the club hosting it?

  • Speechcraft provides a low-risk introduction to Toastmasters for new members.

  • Participants can experience multiple meetings and gain skills quickly.

  • Clubs gain a direct pipeline of engaged new members, especially if the program is tied to membership dues and scheduled during regular meetings.

Attracting participants

A marketing plan helps your club attract participants to your Speechcraft offering.  Get marketing ideas from Sheryl Rousch's Club Marketing handout.

Your marketing plan should include:

  • Who are you marketing to?  Choose a specific audience such as “Young professionals age 20-35 working office jobs at small companies”.

  • What information will you include in the marketing?  Make sure it would appeal to your intended audience.

  • What channels will you leverage for the marketing? Examples: physical postings, social media (LinkedIn, Meetup, etc), word of mouth

  • What is your timeframe for marketing?  When will you start marketing and for how long will the campaign last?

  • A sample of your marketing material such as a flier, a “sales pitch”, or a proposed social media post

  • Who will be the designated coordinator?
